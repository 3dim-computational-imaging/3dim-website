"Last Thursday was a special day as we celebrated the Yavapai-Apache Tribe at the Eller College of Management as part of the ongoing Native Languages of Arizona Campus Sign Initiative.

'Goyá hí, iit’in goshú yú got Teet'
Wisdom Guides You Along the True Path
- Yavapai-Apache Nation (Former Chairman Vincent Randall)

This event was very meaningful as we had the chance to hear from Yavapai-Apache Nation's Chairman Elect Buddy Rocha, who is also a proud Eller Alumni. Thank you to all our speakers, singers, and attendees for making this event so special. We are deeply grateful to the Yavapal-Apache Tribe, university partners, and all who helped bring this event to life.

For more information on the Native Language Sign Initiative, visit our website or email us at tribalengagement@arizona.edu."
 
- Native American Advancement & Tribal Engagement | @uanaate

Land Acknowledgment

We respectfully acknowledge the University of Arizona is on the land and territories of Indigenous peoples. Today, Arizona is home to 22 federally recognized tribes, with Tucson being home to the O’odham and the Yaqui. The university strives to build sustainable relationships with sovereign Native Nations and Indigenous communities through education offerings, partnerships, and community service.
